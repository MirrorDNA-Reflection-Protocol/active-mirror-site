The brief is the product before the video.

MirrorProd is not a random clip generator. It starts by locking the business answer the video must carry, then shapes the visual direction around that answer.

Audience

Who should act after watching: walk-in buyer, repeat customer, investor, parent, learner, or local lead.

Claim source

The offer, fact, menu item, testimonial, price, result, or promise that can safely appear on screen.

Guardrails

Claims to avoid, words that must stay out, sensitive details, and any brand tone that should not be crossed.

Format

Story, reel, launch piece, regional language pack, internal training clip, or product-focused short.

Delivery target

Instagram, WhatsApp, YouTube Shorts, a sales deck, a hiring page, a shop screen, or an event invite.

What gets locked before production

  • The exact audience and business action.
  • The strongest safe claim and the source behind it.
  • What the video must never imply.
  • The final format and where it will be used.

Three clean ways to use it.

Pick the smallest package that matches the business moment. The input can be scrappy; the output still has to look intentional.

Starter Reel

A single focused short for one product, service, location, person, or offer.

  • One brief lock
  • One vertical video direction
  • Caption and CTA line

Campaign Pack

A small set of linked videos for a launch, sale, hiring push, event, or content sprint.

  • Shared campaign message
  • Multiple short formats
  • Reusable copy angles

Regional Pack

A business message shaped for local language, cultural context, and platform behavior.

  • Hindi or English-first direction
  • Local tone and audience fit
  • WhatsApp-ready summary
