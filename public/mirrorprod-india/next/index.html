Certificate of Provenance

  1. Visible AI label on the video — the on-screen disclosure India's IT Rules require, styled, not slapped on.
  2. Provenance metadata embedded in the file — machine-readable origin, traceable end to end.
  3. Consent receipt — when a real face or voice appears, the signed consent artifact travels with the project.
Cert № MP-2026-····Active Mirror · Trust by Design
Why this matters now

Every video leaves here legal to run.

Since February 2026, realistic AI video published in India must be labelled and provenance-tagged. Most services leave that to you. Here it's the delivery standard — like a censor certificate, but for the AI age, and it's yours to keep.

If your face is in the film, the founder lane adds consent custody: captured with explicit consent, used only per approval, receipted on every reuse.

Rules status as of 12 Jul 2026 — labelling and provenance obligations effective 20 Feb 2026; final label-visibility norms still in consultation. A delivery standard, not legal advice.

Before the first brief

Asked, answered.

What kinds of videos can MirrorProd make?

Founder videos, product ads, explainers, awareness campaigns, creator content, local-business promos, and lighter spoof-style social cuts.

Do I need to be good on camera?

No. The point is to remove the pressure of shoots, camera setups, and on-camera performance just to get useful video made.

What does the certificate actually cover?

The visible AI label and embedded provenance metadata India's IT Rules ask for on realistic synthetic media, plus a consent receipt when a real person's likeness is used. You publish without retrofitting compliance.
